iDentity

Resources for Hampstead School students learning HTML and CSS at the British Museum Samsung Centre

The MuseumXDesign Project is centred around identity and how it connects to the past.

On the first day...

...we brainstormed on what identity means and how we identify ourselves.

On the second day...

...we brought in the item we consider the most important to us. I chose my ipad. We looked around the Korea and had to choose an item that related to our own object. I chose ancient Korean coins. We then took photos of both objects and of ourselves.

On the third day...

...we used photoshop to create an image/poster, incorperating our photography into it.

It was facinating to do this because you never really stop and consider how technology of today can relate to something from an Ancient Era.

So how do Korean Mun (ancient coins) relate to iPads?

These coins are circular with a square hole in the middle. My friend noticed the visual connection since the coing looks like a home button or the lens of a camera (which was her item)

  • Both items are made of metal
  • They have the symbol of their creators, which are easy to reconise

The connection went furthur when we realised that having an iPad or a HD camera is a sign of wealth. Apple products are representative of expensive technology, which is why ancient currency links with our items.

Not to mention, coins like Korean mun that had holes in them could be worn together as a necklace. It's a status sign that can't be missed, just like our technology in modern day.

It's just another thing that makes up your identity.
